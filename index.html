EASTERN TIME ZONE

⏱️ 30 min🍽️ Serves 2🔥 steamed + grilled

Maine lobster + Bahamian crawfish (spiny lobster) — two coasts meet: buttery steamed Maine tail meets smoky grilled Bahamian crawfish. Served with citrus-herb compound butter, lemon-garlic asparagus, and classic peas & rice.

📋 INGREDIENT LIST

  • 2 Maine lobster tails (4-5 oz each) – fresh or frozen/thawed
  • 2 Bahamian crawfish (spiny lobster) tails, split lengthwise
  • 1 bunch fresh asparagus (thin spears)
  • 1 cup pigeon peas (or black-eyed peas), cooked
  • 1½ cups jasmine rice
  • 2 cups coconut milk + 1 cup water (for rice)
  • 6 tbsp unsalted butter, room temp
  • Zest & juice of 1 lime + 1 lemon
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tsp fresh thyme leaves
  • ½ tsp Andros goat pepper (or scotch bonnet, optional heat)
  • Salt, black pepper, smoked paprika
  • Fresh parsley & chives for garnish

👨‍🍳 STEP-BY-STEP

  • 1. Prep rice & peas: In a pot, sauté 1 tbsp butter, add rice, stir for 1 min. Add coconut milk, water, pigeon peas, ½ tsp thyme, salt. Bring to boil, cover, reduce heat to low and cook 18 min. Fluff with fork.
  • 2. Compound butter: Mash butter with lime zest & juice, lemon zest (reserve half lemon for asparagus), minced garlic, ½ tsp smoked paprika, pinch of goat pepper, chopped parsley. Roll in plastic and chill.
  • 3. Steam Maine tails: Place Maine tails in a steamer basket over simmering water (season water with salt & bay leaf). Steam 6-8 min until shells turn red and meat is opaque. Brush with 1 tbsp compound butter.
  • 4. Grill Bahamian crawfish: Heat grill/grill pan to medium-high. Brush crawfish flesh with oil, season salt & pepper. Grill flesh-side down 2 min, flip and cook 1 more min. Glaze with compound butter.
  • 5. Asparagus: In a skillet, heat 1 tbsp butter, add asparagus, squeeze of lemon, salt, pepper. Sauté 3-4 min until bright green.
  • 6. Plate: Mound rice in center, arrange both lobster tails, asparagus on side. Top seafood with remaining compound butter, sprinkle fresh chives. Serve with extra lime wedges.
✨ Chef's secret: Use an immersion blender to turn leftover butter + citrus into a quick hollandaise-style drizzle. Ask our team for the technique!

ANCHORAGE TO PUNTA ARENAS

⏱️ 40 min🍽️ Serves 2🔥 cedar plank + seared scallops

Peruvian scallops + Alaskan salmon (cedar plank method) — wood-fired salmon, seared scallops with avocado crema, quick pickled rhubarb, and a Chilean pinot noir reduction. A journey from Anchorage to Punta Arenas on one plate.

📋 INGREDIENT LIST

  • 2 Alaskan salmon fillets (6 oz each), skin on
  • 6 large Peruvian or dry-packed sea scallops
  • 1 cedar grilling plank (soaked 1 hour)
  • Avocado Crema: 2 ripe avocados, ½ cup sour cream, 1 tbsp lime juice, ¼ cup cilantro, 1 jalapeño (optional), salt
  • Pickled Rhubarb: 1 cup rhubarb (thinly sliced), ½ cup apple cider vinegar, ¼ cup sugar, ½ tsp salt, 1 star anise
  • Chilean Pinot Sauce: 1 cup Pinot Noir (Chilean), ½ cup beef/chicken broth, 2 shallots, 1 tbsp butter, fresh thyme
  • 2 cups Miner's lettuce or mixed spring greens
  • Olive oil, salt, smoked paprika

👨‍🍳 STEP-BY-STEP

  • 1. Pickled rhubarb: Heat vinegar, sugar, salt, star anise until dissolved. Pour over sliced rhubarb in a bowl; let sit 20 min, then drain.
  • 2. Avocado crema: Blend avocados, sour cream, lime juice, cilantro, jalapeño (optional) with 2 tbsp water until smooth. Season salt. Refrigerate.
  • 3. Cedar plank salmon: Preheat grill to medium-high. Place soaked plank, close lid, heat 3 min. Rub salmon with oil, salt, smoked paprika. Place on plank skin-side down, close grill, cook 12-15 min until flaky.
  • 4. Pinot sauce: In a pan, sauté shallots in butter, add Pinot Noir, reduce by half. Add broth, thyme sprigs, reduce until syrupy (5 min). Strain, keep warm.
  • 5. Sear scallops: Pat scallops dry, season with salt. Heat cast-iron skillet, add oil, sear scallops 90 secs per side until golden brown. Rest on paper towel.
  • 6. Assembly: Place lettuce on plates. Arrange salmon and scallops, drizzle pinot sauce around, dollop avocado crema, scatter pickled rhubarb. Use squeeze bottles for stripe effect (green crema + red wine sauce).
🔥 Pro trick: Soak plank in apple cider + water for extra aroma. We stock quality planks and wild Alaskan salmon — just ask!

TIERRA DEL FUEGO

⏱️ 35 min🍽️ Serves 2🔥 surf & turf

Chilean sea bass + Patagonia-style beef tenderloin — buttery, miso-marinated sea bass meets grilled grass-fed beef, paired with Brazilian-leaning chimichurri and a calafate berry glaze. A taste of the world's end.

📋 INGREDIENT LIST

  • 2 Chilean sea bass fillets (6 oz each)
  • 2 beef tenderloin medallions (4-5 oz each, 1.5" thick)
  • Brazilian Chimichurri: 1 cup fresh parsley, ½ cup cilantro, 4 garlic cloves, ½ cup olive oil, 2 tbsp red wine vinegar, 1 tsp malagueta chili (or red pepper flakes), 1 tsp smoked paprika, pinch of cumin
  • Calafate Berry Glaze: ¼ cup calafate berry jam (or dark berry jam + dash of blackcurrant), 1 tbsp balsamic vinegar, 1 tsp honey
  • Miso marinade for bass: 1 tbsp white miso, 1 tbsp mirin, 1 tsp sesame oil, 1 tsp ginger
  • 1 tbsp smoked salt, cracked black pepper
  • Grilled patty pan squash or roasted fingerling potatoes (optional side)

👨‍🍳 STEP-BY-STEP

  • 1. Chimichurri: Finely chop parsley, cilantro, garlic. Mix with olive oil, vinegar, malagueta, paprika, cumin. Let sit 20 min (room temp).
  • 2. Marinate sea bass: Whisk miso, mirin, sesame oil, grated ginger. Coat bass fillets, marinate 15 min.
  • 3. Calafate glaze: Warm jam with balsamic and honey until pourable. Set aside.
  • 4. Sear beef: Pat tenderloin dry, season generously with smoked salt & pepper. Heat cast-iron skillet with oil, sear 2-3 min each side for medium-rare. Rest 5 min.
  • 5. Cook sea bass: Heat non-stick pan over medium-high, scrape excess marinade. Sear bass skin-side down 3-4 min until crisp, flip and cook 2 more min (bass is delicate, baste with butter).
  • 6. Plate: Slice beef tenderloin. Arrange bass and beef on warm plates. Spoon chimichurri over beef, drizzle calafate glaze around sea bass. Garnish with smoked salt flakes and microgreens. Serve with Patagonia-inspired roasted potatoes.
🧉 Legend says: If you taste calafate berry, you’ll return to Patagonia. We can't guarantee your return ticket, but this recipe will bring you back to our shop. Ask for our house-smoked salt!
